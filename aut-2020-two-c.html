Autumn 2020 Question 2(c)

Starting from the Black-Scholes formula for the price \(C(t)\) of a call option, use put- call parity to derive the Black-Scholes formula for the price \( P(t)\) of the corresponding put option.

$$(C(t,S(t)) = S(t)\Phi(d_1) - Ke^{-r(T-t)}\Phi(d2)$$

Put-Call Parity tells us that \(S(t) + P (t) − C(t) = Ke^{ −r(T −t)}\)

From this, we can say \(S(t) + P(t) − C(t,S(t)) = Ke^{ −r(T −t)}\)

$$S(t) + P(t) - (C(t,S(t)) = S(t) + P(t) - S(t)\Phi(d_1) - Ke^{-r(T-t)}\Phi(d2) = Ke^{-r(T-t)} $$

$$ S(t)(1- \Phi(d_1)) + P(t)= Ke^{-r(T-t)}(1 + \Phi(d2)) $$

$$P(t,S(t)) = Ke^{-r(T-t)}(1 + \Phi(d2)) - S(t)(1- \Phi(d_1)) $$
