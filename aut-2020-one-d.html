Autumn 2020 Question 1(d)

The current share price of a company is €50. The share price has volatility 20%. The risk-free interest rate is 5% per annum. Use the Black-Scholes Formula to compute the price of a European call option on the stock with strike price €53 that expires in three months’ time.

The Black-Scholes Formula for a European Call Option on an asset whose price at time \(t\) is\( S(t)\)

$$(C(t,S(t)) = S(t)\Phi(d_1) - Ke^{-r(T-t)}\Phi(d2)$$

Where \(K\) is the strike price, \(r\) is the continuously compounded risk-free interest rate, \(\sigma\) is the volatility of the asset,

$$d_1 = {log(S(t)/K)+(r+{1 \over 2}\sigma^2)(T-t) \over \sigma \sqrt{T-t}}$$

\(d_2 = d_1 - \sigma \sqrt{T-t}\) and \(\Phi\) is the cumulative distribution function for the standard normal distribution

Figures for this question

$$\sigma = 0.2$$

$$S(0) = €50$$

$$K = €53$$

$$T-t = {3 \over 12}$$

So for this question, \(d_1\) =

$${ln({50 \over 53})+(0.05+{1 \over 2}0.2^2)({3 \over 12}) \over 0.2\sqrt{3 \over 12}}$$

$$= -0.407689$$

This subsequently means...

$$d_2 = -0.407689 - 0.2\sqrt{3 \over 12} = -0.507689 $$

We Return now to \((C(t,S(t)) = S(t)\Phi(d_1) - Ke^{-r(T-t)}\Phi(d2)\)

$$= 50\Phi(-0.40769) - 53e^{-0.05({3 \over 12})}\Phi(-0.506789)$$

Using Log Tables

$$=50(0.3409) -53(0.9876)(0.3050)$$

$$=€1.08$$
